Hi, I’m James—welcome to my UNCG food blog

I’m James Wilds III, a senior Computer Science major at UNCG in Greensboro. Between classes in the Petty building, I’m always hunting for quick, tasty spots that won’t derail my study schedule. This blog rounds up a few of my go-to places within an easy half-mile walk from Petty, plus three specific menu items I recommend at each. I’ve kept the layout simple, semantic, and accessible so it’s easy to skim on your phone or laptop. If you’re cramming for exams or grabbing a bite after lab, these picks should help.

Tate Street Coffee House

Vibe: Classic campus coffee spot great for study breaks.

Where: Tate Street, ~5–10 minute walk from Petty.

Why I like it: Dependable espresso, quick breakfast bites, friendly staff.

Latte

Smooth and balanced—my pre-class caffeine pick-me-up.

Toasted Bagel

Simple, fast, and budget-friendly breakfast option.

Blueberry Muffin

Lightly sweet; pairs perfectly with a drip coffee.

Boba House

Vibe: Vegetarian Vietnamese & Thai favorites with lots of flavor.

Where: Tate Street area, easy walk from Petty.

Why I like it: Great textures, cozy space, and solid takeout.

Fresh Spring Rolls

Crisp and refreshing—ideal starter between lectures.

Pho

Comforting broth and noodles—perfect on rainy Greensboro days.

Tofu Curry

Creamy, aromatic, and filling without being heavy.

Sushi Republic

Vibe: Casual sushi spot—good for quick rolls or a relaxed dinner.

Where: Near campus within a half-mile of Petty.

Why I like it: Consistent quality and friendly service.

Spicy Tuna Roll

A dependable classic with a little kick.

Teriyaki Bowl

Hearty, savory, and easy to take to-go.

Mochi

Chewy, lightly sweet finish after a study session.

Side Notes & Deals

Some spots run student specials—always ask at the counter and show your UNCG ID. Also, if you’re using Flex or dining dollars, double-check where they’re accepted each term because policies can change.
